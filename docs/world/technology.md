# Technology Progression

How the technological landscape changes over time — from pure legacy dependence toward local production capability, and how the player and factions influence that trajectory.

---

## Existing foundation

The economy has the two-tier commodity model (local vs. legacy). This design defines how the boundary between the tiers shifts.

---

## Design

Technology progression is not a tech tree or a research screen. It's an economic process. There's no single point where a legacy commodity "becomes" local — "local" just means "produced industrially on Mars." The question is always cost and quality, not locked/unlocked.

### How it works

A node with the right infrastructure (fabrication line + raw materials + skilled workforce + power) can attempt to produce a legacy commodity locally. Early output is expensive, slow, and low-quality. Over time, as quanta with relevant proficiencies work the line, output improves. The quality of locally produced goods scales continuously with workforce proficiency and infrastructure condition.

### Knowledge spread

Progression is not global. Manufacturing knowledge spreads the way intel does — through movement of skilled workers. A trained fabrication technician who moves from one settlement to another carries proficiency with them. This means progress diffuses across a region over time, but it's not instant and it can be concentrated or disrupted. Poaching skilled workers is a viable strategy. So is killing them.

### Player influence

No direct research interface. The player influences progression purely through economic actions: delivering rare materials to a production node, protecting supply lines, capturing intact legacy equipment for reverse-engineering, or disrupting a rival's manufacturing capability. The player sees the effects (component quality improving, prices dropping) without a progress bar.

### Timescale

A full transition for a single commodity (from legacy-only to mature local production at one node) might take an entire playthrough. Maybe one or two commodities get meaningfully bootstrapped per game. This means the legacy stockpile countdown remains a real pressure throughout the campaign — you can't just research your way out of scarcity.

### Interruption

Destroy the fabrication infrastructure and production stops. Kill the skilled workers and even intact equipment operates at reduced quality. Progression is fragile. This makes industrial targets strategically valuable — you're not just taking stuff, you're destroying capability.

---

## What's new

- Production quality modifier on recipes: output quality scales continuously with workforce proficiency and infrastructure condition
- Component quality as a continuous value: components built with crude local materials have lower maxHP/hardness than legacy equivalents, with the gap narrowing over time
- Workforce proficiency tracking per node per commodity type
- Knowledge diffusion: worker movement spreads manufacturing capability between nodes
